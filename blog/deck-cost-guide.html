Deck Cost Calculator: How Much Does a Deck Cost to Build?

6 min read · Outdoor

A deck is one of the best home improvement investments, typically returning 60–80% of its cost in added home value while giving you years of outdoor living enjoyment. Here's how to budget your deck project accurately.

Average Deck Costs

Deck TypeCost per Square FootTypical Total Cost
Pressure-treated wood$15–$25$5,000–$15,000
Cedar or redwood$25–$35$8,000–$20,000
Composite decking$30–$60$12,000–$35,000
PVC decking$35–$70$15,000–$40,000
Hardwood (Ipe, Teak)$25–$50$10,000–$30,000

Decking Material Comparison

MaterialLifespanMaintenanceBest For
Pressure-treated pine15–20 yearsAnnual sealingBudget projects
Cedar20–30 yearsStaining every 2–3 yearsNatural look
Composite25–30 yearsMinimal — occasional cleaningLow maintenance
PVC30+ yearsVery minimalDurability

Additional Deck Costs

  • Railing — $30–$100 per linear foot depending on material
  • Stairs — $400–$1,500 per staircase
  • Footings and posts — $100–$200 each (need one every 6–8 feet)
  • Permit — $200–$1,000 depending on size and location
  • Lighting — $500–$2,000 for deck lighting
Pro Tip: Composite decking costs 2–3x more upfront than pressure-treated wood, but saves money long-term — no staining, no sealing, no splinters, and it won't warp or rot.

Do You Need a Permit for a Deck?

Most jurisdictions require a permit for any deck over 30 inches off the ground or over a certain square footage (often 200 sq ft). Always check with your local building department. An unpermitted deck can create issues during home sales and may not meet safety codes.
