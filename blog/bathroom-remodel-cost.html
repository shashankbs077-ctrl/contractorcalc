Bathroom Remodel Cost: How to Budget Your Project

6 min read · Remodeling

A bathroom remodel is one of the best investments you can make in your home — it improves daily life and adds resale value. But costs range from $3,000 for a cosmetic refresh to $30,000+ for a luxury gut renovation. Here's how to budget accurately.

Average Bathroom Remodel Costs

Remodel TypeAverage Cost Range
Cosmetic update (paint, fixtures, accessories)$1,000–$5,000
Mid-range remodel (new tile, vanity, tub/shower)$8,000–$15,000
Full gut renovation$15,000–$30,000+
Luxury master bath$30,000–$75,000+

Cost Breakdown by Component

ItemTypical Cost
Labor (total)40–65% of total budget
Shower/tub replacement$1,500–$6,000
Vanity and sink$500–$3,000
Toilet$200–$800
Tile (floor and walls)$1,000–$4,000
Lighting$200–$1,000
Plumbing changes$500–$3,500

What Drives Up Cost the Most

  • Moving plumbing — Relocating a toilet or shower drain can add $1,000–$5,000
  • Water damage repair — Hidden mold or rot discovered during demo adds unexpected cost
  • Custom tile work — Complex patterns and large-format tiles take more labor time
  • Heated floors — Radiant floor heating adds $500–$1,500 to a bathroom
Pro Tip: Keep plumbing in the same location to save 20–30% on your bathroom remodel. Moving the toilet across the room can add more cost than the toilet itself.

How to Save Money on a Bathroom Remodel

  • Refinish the tub instead of replacing it — saves $500–$1,500
  • Keep the layout the same — avoid moving plumbing
  • Install a vanity yourself — it's one of the easier DIY tasks
  • Choose stock tile over custom — similar look at 30–50% less cost
  • Get 3+ contractor quotes and check references
