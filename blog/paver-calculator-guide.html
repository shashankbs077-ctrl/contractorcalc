Paver Calculator: Estimate Pavers for Patios and Paths

5 min read · Landscaping

A paver patio or walkway adds curb appeal and outdoor living space to your home. Getting your material quantities right upfront prevents costly mid-project supply runs. Here's how to calculate everything you need.

Step 1: Calculate Your Patio Area

Measure the length and width of your planned patio in feet. Multiply to get square footage. Add 10% for cuts and waste, especially for curved edges or patterns like herringbone.

Step 2: Calculate Number of Pavers

Divide your total square footage (including waste) by the square footage of one paver.

Common paver sizes: 4×8 inches (0.22 sq ft), 6×6 inches (0.25 sq ft), 12×12 inches (1.0 sq ft), 16×16 inches (1.78 sq ft)

DIY Paver Installation Tips

  • Excavate 8–10 inches deep to accommodate all layers
  • Compact the gravel base thoroughly — rent a plate compactor
  • Use screed pipes to level your sand bed before laying pavers
  • Install edge restraints on all sides to keep pavers from shifting
  • Seal pavers 30 days after installation to protect color and prevent staining
